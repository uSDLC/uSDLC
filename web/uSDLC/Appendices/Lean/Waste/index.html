Eliminate Waste (Muda)

Eight ways to increase project overheads

Lean defines 7 kinds of waste - DOTWIMP to which we can add a U:

  • Defects - called bugs - cause waste to find and fix - more waste the later found or when they re-emerge.
  • Overproduction - extra features - that are not needed or will never be used. Over-generalisation of packages.
  • Transportation - hand-offs - Vision holder to analysts to architects to developers to testers to support.
  • Waiting - delays waiting for information or decisions from other groups
  • Inventory - partially completed work costs more to restart and complete
  • Motion - task switching - the cost of interruption
  • Processing - procedures that do nothing and writing documents that are not used
  • Under-utilisation (of people).
