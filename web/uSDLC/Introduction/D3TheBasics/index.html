The Basics

of Document Driven Development

Discard your preconceptions. Document Driven Development (D3) is not about having to write or read reams of documents. It is about the glue that binds the life-cycle of a project together to make everyone’s job a little easier.

For D3 to be feasible it needs a supporting system similar to a content management system or wiki. It differs in that it requires that every paragraph, every spreadsheet and all supporting data to be activated. This means that they will be attached to supporting code that will exercise the related areas in the system being developed.

Management at last get what they have always wanted – cohesive complete documents that remain up-to-date for the life of the project.

Project Managers will be able to extract meaningful statistics to measure the progress of a project – even when that project is completely agile.

Business Analysts have a medium to store the results of their studies in a way that will ensure that the implemented system meets the requirements they have uncovered.

Architects can be assured that their designs and documentation will be used as every paragraph is flagged until activated.

Team Leads have clear reports on what work is to be done with sufficient detail for implementation. Unstarted and assigned sections are marked for report but not activated during continuous integration.

Developers can work to clear requirements and know when a task is done.

Test Personnel are involved much earlier in the project to help analysts and architects to activate the document as it is being produced.

Technical Writers have all the information they need to produce supporting documentation. They can also be involved earlier in the project to own the clarity of the D3 document.

Support Staff have clear documented and tested systems that lend themselves to safe support and extension.

D3 supports lean and agile methodologies across the software development life-cycle. This is much more powerful that limiting those methodologies to the build phase alone. D3 breaks down the barriers between design, implementation and release.
