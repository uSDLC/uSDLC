uSDLC Elemental Template

SDLC Phases in an Agile framework

This project template follows the traditional software development life-cycle phases fairly closely, while expecting an agile iterative approach to implementation.

The Vision

The project owner is often, but not always the vision holder. The vision holder should create and maintain this section. While most of the work recording the vision will be complete before the project starts, updates should be made as the vision matures with the project. Enter your project vision as a short clear paragraph and use the link above to provide more structured details.

The Business Case

Having a vision is a wonderful thing. Making it reality is hard work. The first step is about viability.

A business case is the information that describes the justification for a project. A business case is built on evidence derived from feasibility studies or impact analysis to support project objectives. (HRSDC)

Requirements Gathering

In requirements engineering, requirements elicitation is the practice of obtaining the requirements of a system from users, customers and other stakeholders. The practice is also sometimes referred to as requirements gathering. (Wikipedia)

Business Analysis

Define how the system should look and behave based on the requirements gathered in (2). The result is the input and output of the system. Screens as wire-frames or prototypes, reports and other interfaces to humans or other systems. Client interaction is reduced to about 10% and involves mostly review.

Business analysis is the discipline of identifying business needs and determining solutions to business problems. Solutions often include a systems development component, but may also consist of process improvement, organizational change or strategic planning and policy development. (Wikipedia)

Technical Architecture

The TA is often completed by a technical BA. It is about reviewing the technical environment for the system. It is about the hardware, network and physical interfaces to other systems. The challenge is to fit into the existing systems where possible and to define the needs for extension.

Technical architecture breaks down operational (business) processes into functional components and capabilities (WTEC)

Software Architecture

The structure of a piece of software, comprised of a hierarchy of patterns (.Berkeley)

 

Software Design

Given the information and constraints in the above sections design the software system. This consists of component descriptions and relationships, component interface definitions and recommendations of proof of concept code for the more difficult sections. At a minimum the output is a block diagram and a design document. At the most it is empty classes that define both the interface and functionality for the system down to a defined level of complexity.

Software design is a process of problem-solving and planning for a software solution. After the purpose and specifications of software are determined, software developers will design or employ designers to develop a plan for a solution. (Wikipedia)

Software Build

Computer programming (often shortened to programming or coding) is the process of designing, writing, testing, debugging / troubleshooting, and maintaining the source code of computer programs. (Wikipedia)

Verification

This section is most commonly called testing. As testing is now being considered  to start  earlier in the SDLC, we call this section verification. Here representatives of the client verify that the system as it is to be delivered meets expectations.

Software testing is an investigation conducted to provide stakeholders with information about the quality of the product or service under test. (Wikipedia)

Data Migration

At this point in history there are very few truly green-field projects. Even systems that meet new needs will need to interface with or consume information from older systems.

 The periodic transfer of data from one hardware or software configuration to another, or from one generation of computer technology to a subsequent generation. Migration is a necessary action for retaining the integrity of the data and for allowing users to search, retrieve, and make use of data in the face of constantly changing technology. (uTexas)

Release

Release includes data migration, production installation, final verification, change-over and retirement of the old system. It also includes planning for change management.

Support

Once a system goes live it is typically handed over to a support team. Support involves bug fixes to the production system as problems are discovered.

Minor Upgrades

Release deadlines often leave less critical parts of the system unimplemented or ready for release. Sometimes a project plans a suite of upgrades. System use often leads to the discovery of additional useful or even critical functionality.

End of Life

At some point the system will become difficult or impossible to upgrade to meet the growing needs of the client. Other reasons for reaching end of life include

  • Aging hardware that cannot be replaced with a compatible system.
  • Security concerns.
  • Infrastructure, application server or database upgrades that are not compatible with the existing system.
  • Inability to find support staff or upgrade developers willing to admit to being able to support the old technology.
  • New technologies such as client-server to web or mobile device support.
