Who Benefits

Product owner receives a more reliable result that more closely matches the vision. With full transparency, discrepencies can be identified and dealt with early.

The project manager can review progress and assign priorities as easily for agile as for waterfall projects.

Business analysts can record their findings and examples in such a way that they will be used to test the production code - ensuring that learnings at the analysis phase will result in matching production code.

A product designer will have all the analysis discoveries and example, and can build them into the solution. 

Architects will be able to tie requirements documents to instrumentation to ensure that architectural needs are not forgotten in the production code.

The team lead can prioritise and assign work directly from uSDLC. Completed tasks show up as soon as they are checked in.

The Software Designer has the information on hand for high and low level designs. The results include generating scenarios the will keep the design in line with earlier stake-holders and the resulting production system.

Developers will be presented with a clear task. Once a scenario is implemented, the task is complete. If additional information is required, the task can be followed up the tree all the way back to the vision to find details - and who specified said detail.

Test specialists have more information to work with. The task is more about creating scenarios to fill gaps than repetitive testing.

The technical writer can spend more time writing and less time having to troll for the required information from other teams. All the information will be available in uSDLC. It needs to be organised and clarified for training, guides and other technical documents.

Support teams will have a full picture of the software they are supporting. They will be able to run the instrumentation to ensure continued correct operation after changes. They can add scenarios to cover post-release problems so that once they are fixed they won't resurface.
